# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

SMB founders, product managers, and GTM operators at 10-200 person companies who are losing deals to competitors they didn't see coming. They're not enterprise analysts with Crayon licenses. They're operators who need to know what their competitors changed, hired, or priced, and what to do about it. Time-poor, skeptical of fluff, want signal not noise.

## Product Purpose

Vigil automates competitive intelligence so SMBs don't fly blind. Scrapes competitor websites, job boards, reviews, and ad campaigns, runs it through an AI pipeline, delivers a weekly executive brief plus real-time alerts. The landing page must convert skeptical visitors who've been burned by overpromising SaaS tools.

## Brand Personality

Bold, aggressive, technical. Direct, confident, occasionally blunt. "We catch what you miss" not "leverage our synergistic intelligence platform."

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with gradient blobs, smiling people illustrations, "Trusted by 10,000+ teams"
- Overdesigned heroes with floating 3D elements and particle effects
- Corporate tone ("We help businesses unlock their competitive potential")
- TemplateMonster / ThemeForest template look
- Stripe.com clone minimalism
- AI-washing with sparkles and robot icons
- Gradient text (bg-clip-text with gradient)

## Design Principles

1. Show the product, don't describe it. The demo is the hero.
2. Signal over polish. Every element communicates something useful.
3. Confident directness. Headlines sound like a competent analyst.
4. Technical credibility. Built by engineers who ship.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast. Keyboard navigable. Screen reader compatible. Reduced motion via prefers-reduced-motion.
